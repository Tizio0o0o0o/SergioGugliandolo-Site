I hate carrying around a huge wallet that takes space just to have dividers between cards. I only need cards and some small "useful" things.

My idea is to enroll cards in a elastic tessute. No extra space will be occupied and cards and bills will safely be stored thanks to the rubber in the inside of the tissue.

The small things still need a place to stay and adding a key holder might be a plus. So a 3D printed card-shaped object is the perfect solution.

The objects that I'm able to carry in the finished design are:

  • ∘ 2 coins;
  • ∘ 1 female USB-A to male USB-C adapter;
  • ∘ 1 USB stick;
  • ∘ 1 clamp to hold together the last 2 items;
  • ∘ 1 micro SD to USB-C adapter;
  • ∘ 4 micro SD;
  • ∘ 1 SIM tool;
  • ∘ 1 big key;
  • ∘ 2 small keys.

All in 5mm of height (the big key is 5mm thick).

The design is optimized to be 3D printed with a 0.4mm nozzle at layer height 0.2mm
